4 FORWARDS, FUTURES, DIVIDENDS, COMMODITY PRICING AND CONVENIENCE YIELDS

4.1 Forward Contracts

  • \(\underline{w_{t}}\) : information at \(t\)
  • Forward contract : Agreement signed at \(t\) (to-day) to buy/sell an asset (a commodity) at a given delivery date \(T>t\) at a price \(\Phi_{t,T}\) (delivery price or forward price) decided at \(t\).
  • \(S_T :\) value of the asset (commodity) at \(T\), (function of \(\underline{w_{T}}\)).
  • \(S_T - \Phi_{t,T}\): payoff of the contract at \(T\).
  • \(r_t :\) interest rate between \(t\) and \(t+1\) (known at \(t\)), (function of \(\underline{w_{t}}\)).
  • \(B(t_1,t_2-t_1)\): date-\(t_1\) price of a zero-coupon bond whose value is 1 on \(t_2\).

Proposition 4.1 (Forward price) We have: \[\begin{eqnarray*} \Phi_{t,T} & = & \frac{\mathbb{E}_t (M_{t,t+1}, \ldots, M_{T-1,T} S_T)}{B(t,T-t)}\\ & = & \frac{\mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t [\exp (-r_t - \ldots - r_{T-1}) S_T]}{B(t,T-t)}. \end{eqnarray*}\]

:::{.proof} The price at \(t\) of \(S_T - \Phi_{t,T}\) is zero. Therefore: \(\mathbb{E}_t (M_{t,t+1}, \ldots, M_{T-1,T} S_T) - \Phi_{t,T} \mathbb{E}_t (M_{t,t+1} \ldots M_{T-1,T}) = 0\). The result follows. :::

  • If the processes \((r_t)\) and \((S_t)\) are \(\mathbb{Q}\)-independent, then \[ \Phi_{t,T} = \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t (S_T) \] and \(\Phi_{t,T}\) is a \(\mathbb{Q}\)-martingale.

  • If the asset does not generate any payoff before \(T\) (no dividends), then \[ \Phi_{t,T} = \frac{S_t}{B(t,T-t)}. \]

  • The price at \(s\), with \(t<s<T\), of a forward contract signed at \(t\) is: \[ (\Phi_{s,T} - \Phi_{t,T}) B (s,T-s), \] since the payoff \(S_T - \Phi_{t,T}\) at \(T\) has this price at \(s\).

  • Notations associated with dividends:

  • \(\tilde{S}_t\): ex-dividend price at \(t\),

  • \(S_t\): cum-dividend price at \(t\),

  • \(S_t = \tilde{S}_t \exp (\delta_t)\), \(\delta_t :\) dividend yield (or rate) known at \(t\).

  • We get: \[\begin{eqnarray*} \tilde{S}_{T-1} & = & \mathbb{E}_{T-1} (M_{T-1,T} S_T) \\ \mbox{or } \\ S_{T-1} & = & \mathbb{E}_{T-1} [M_{T-1,T} \exp (\delta_{T-1}) S_T], \end{eqnarray*}\] and, recursively: \[\begin{eqnarray*} S_t & = & \mathbb{E}_t [M_{t,t+1} \ldots M_{T-1,T} \exp (\delta_t + \ldots + \delta_{T-1}) S_T] \\ &=& \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t [\exp (-r_t \ldots - r_{T-1} + \delta_t + \ldots + \delta_{T-1}) S_T]. \end{eqnarray*}\]

\(\Rightarrow\) \(r_t\) replaced by \(r_t - \delta_t\).

  • Replacing \(S_t\) by \(\tilde{S} \exp(\delta_t)\) and \(S_T\) by \(\tilde{S}_T \exp (\delta_T)\), we get \[ \tilde{S}_t = \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t [\exp (-r_t -\ldots-r_{T-1} + \delta_{t+1} + \ldots + \delta_T) \tilde{S}_T. \]
  • If deterministic \(\delta_t\): \[ \Phi_{t,T} = \frac{S_t \exp (-\delta_t - \ldots - \delta_{T-1})}{B(t,T-t)}. \] If \(r_t\) deterministic too: \[ \Phi_{t,T} = S_t \exp (r_t + \ldots + r_{T-1} - \delta_t - \ldots - \delta_{T-1}). \]

4.2 Futures Contracts, Futures Prices

  • Futures contract: agreement signed at \(t\) to buy/sell an asset (a commodity) at given delivery date \(T>t\) at a price \(F_{t,T}\) (futures price) decided at \(t\).
  • Difference with forward contract: both counterparties are required to deposit into a margin account at every trading day \(s>t\) the resettlement payment (margin call). The latter is equal to: \[ \Delta_{s,T} = F_{s,T} - F_{s-1,T} \quad \mbox{(for the buyer)}. \]

\(\Rightarrow\) A Futures contract is actually closed out after every day, starts afresh the next day and therefore is valued zero.

Proposition 4.2 (Pricing futures) We have: \[ F_{t,T} = \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t (S_T), \] that is \(F_{t,T}\) is a \(\mathbb{Q}\)-martingale, or \(\Delta_{t,T}\) is a \(\mathbb{Q}\)-martingale difference.

Proof. At each date \(s\ge t\), after the deposit of the resettlement payment, there is a new contract valued zero and paying \(F_{s+1,T} - F_{s,T}\) at \(s+1\) (and providing another zero valued contract at \(s+1\)). Therefore \(0 = \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_s [\exp (-r_s) (F_{s+1,T} - F_{s,T})]\), and \(0 = \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_s (F_{s+1,T} - F_{s,T})\) since \(\exp (-r_s)\) is known at \(s\). Hence \(F_{s,T} = \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_s (F_{s+1,T})\), and the results follows from \(F_{T,T} = S_T\).

Proposition 4.3 (XXXX) \[ \Phi_{t,T} - F_{t,T} = \frac{cov^{\mathbb{Q}}_t \left[\prod^{T-1}_{s=t} \exp (-r_s), S_T\right]}{B(t,T-t)}. \]

Proof. We have: \[\begin{eqnarray*} \Phi_{t,T} - F_{t,T} & = & \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t \left[\frac{\prod^{T-1}_{s=t} \exp (-r_s) S_T }{B(t,T-t)}- S_T\right] \\ &=& \frac{\mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t \left[\prod^{T-1}_{s=t} \exp (-r_s) S_T\right] - \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t \left[\prod^{T-1}_{s=t} \exp (-r_s)\right] \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t (S_T)}{B(t,T-t)} \\ &=&\frac{cov^{\mathbb{Q}}_t \left[\prod^{T-1}_{s=t} \exp (-r_s), S_T)\right]}{B(t,T-t)}. \end{eqnarray*}\]

  • \(\Phi_{t,T} = F_{t,T}\), if, and only if, \(\prod^{T-1}_{s=t} \exp (-r_s)\) and \(S_T\) are conditionally uncorrelated under \(\mathbb{Q}\). In particular it is true in the case of deterministic short rates.

4.3 Convenience Yields

:::{.definition #convYield name=“Convenience yield”] A convenience yield} is a net benefit associated with holding a physical asset (rather than a forward or futures contract). It is net in the sense that it is equal to the positive gain of holding minus the cost of storage. :::

  • The notion of convenience yield is valid only for storable commodities (not, e.g., for electricity).
  • Can be positive or negative.
  • Notion of convenience yield mathematically similar to a dividend yield (but can be \(<0\), and latent).

the following, we denote the convenience yield by \(c_t\). * The price is here the cum convenience yield price.

Basic pricing approach

  • \(r_t\) and \(c_t\) deterministic. We have: \[ \Phi_{t,T} = F_{t,T} = S_t \exp (r_t + \ldots + r_{T-1} - c_t - \ldots - c_{T-1}). \]
  • Moreover, if \(r_t\) and \(c_t\) time independent: \[ \Phi_{t,T} = F_{t,T} = S_t \exp [(T-t)(r-c)]. \]
  • If \(r>c\) forward curve function \(\uparrow\) of \(T\): situation of contango;
  • If \(r<c\) forward curve function \(\downarrow\) of \(T\): backwardated forward curve.

Advanced approach: \(c_t\) is stochastic.

  • We have: \[\begin{eqnarray} \Phi_{t,T} &=& \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t [\exp (-r_t - \ldots - r_{T-1}) S_T]/B(t,T-t) \tag{4.1}\\ F_{t,T} &=& \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t (S_T) \tag{4.2}\\ S_t &=& \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t [\exp (-r_t - \ldots - r_{T-1} + c_t + \ldots + c_{T-1}) S_T] \tag{4.3}. \end{eqnarray}\]
  • necessitates a joint modelling (at least under \(\mathbb{Q}\)) of: \(S_t\) (or \(s_t = \log S_t\)), \(c_t\) (convenience yield), \(r_t\) (short rate),
  • and, in general, other factors (observable or latent).

Direct or backward approach (see XXX).

  • Case of a nonstorable commodity. No convenience yield; if, moreover \(r_t\) deterministic, \(\Phi_{t,T} = F_{t,T} = \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t (S_T)\), but since Eq. (@ref(eq:convenience3}) is not valid we do not have \(S_t = B(t,T) \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t (S_T)\) and therefore we do not have \(\Phi_{t,T} = S_t / B(t,T)\). \end{remark}

  • Case of a storable commodity The price ex-convenience yield at \(t\), i.e. \(S_t \exp (-c_t)\), is the price at \(t\) of \(S_{t+1}\): \[\begin{eqnarray} S_t \exp (-c_t) &=& \exp (-r_t) \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t (S_{t+1}) \nonumber\\ \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t (S_{t+1}) & = & S_t \exp (r_t - c_t) \tag{4.4} \\ \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t \exp (s_{t+1}) &=& \exp(s_t+r_t-c_t). \nonumber \end{eqnarray}\] Or if \(y_{t+1} = \log \frac{S_{t+1}}{S_t}\) denotes the (geometric) return, then: \[ \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t \exp (y_{t+1}) = \exp (r_t - c_t). \]

  • Eq. (4.3) is automatically satisfied (using Eq. (4.4) recursively).

4.4 Pricing with Affine Models

  • If storable commodity: \[ w_t = (s_t, c_t, r_t, x'_t)', \] where \(s_t = \log S_t\) and \(x_t\) is a vector of additional factors.
  • \(w_t\) \(\mathbb{Q}\)-affine: \[ \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t \exp (u' w_{t+1}) = \exp [a' (u) w_t + b(u)]. \]
  • If \(S_{t+1} = \exp (e_1' w_{t+1})\), ICCs give: \[\begin{eqnarray*} \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t \exp (s_{t+1}) &=& \exp (s_t-c_t+r_t)\\ \Leftrightarrow \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t \exp (e_1' w_{t+1}) &=& \exp [(e_1 - e_2 + e_3)' w_t]\\ \Leftrightarrow && \left\{\begin{array}{lcl} a(e_1) &=& e_1 - e_2 + e_3 \\ b(e_1) & =&0 \end{array} \right. \end{eqnarray*}\] (if non storable, no \(c_t\), no ICCs).
  • Since (Eqs. (??) and (4.2)): \[\begin{eqnarray*} \Phi_{t,T} & = & \frac{\mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t [\exp (-r_t - \ldots - r_{t-1} + s_T)]}{\mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t [\exp (-r_t - \ldots - r_{T-1})]} \\ F_{t,T} & = & \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t [\exp (s_T)], \end{eqnarray*}\] \(\Rightarrow\) quasi explicit formulas using multihorizon Laplace transforms, exponential affine in \(w_t = (s_t, c_t, r_t, x^{'}_t)'\).

4.5 Historical dynamics

  • We have: \[ M_{t,t+1} (\underline{w_{t+1}}) = \exp [-r_t + \alpha'_t w_{t+1} + \psi^{\mathbb{Q}}_t (-\alpha_t)], \] and \[ \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t (M_{t,t+1}^{-1}) = \exp (r_t). \]

  • Historical dynamics defined through: \[ \psi_t (u) = \psi^{\mathbb{Q}}_t (u-\alpha_t) - \psi^{\mathbb{Q}}_t(-\alpha_t). \]

  • Note: no constraint on \(\alpha_t\), possibility to introduce seasonality.

4.6 A Gaussian VAR Model

  • State vector: \(w_t = (s_t, c_t, r_t)'\).

  • R.N. dynamics: \[ w_{t+1} = A_0 + A_1 w_t + \varepsilon_{t+1}, \quad \varepsilon_{t+1} \sim i.i.d. \mathcal{N}(0,\Sigma) \mbox{ under }\mathbb{Q}. \]

  • We have (see Example 1.6): \[\begin{eqnarray*} \mathbb{E}_t \exp (u' w_{t+1}) &=& \exp \left[u' (A_0 + A_1 w_t) + \frac{1}{2} u' \Sigma u\right] \\ \Rightarrow && \left\{ \begin{array}{ccl} a(u) &=&A^{'}_1 u \\ b(u) & =& A'_0 u + \frac{1}{2} u' \Sigma u. \end{array} \right. \end{eqnarray*}\]

  • Let us decompose \(A_0\) and \(A_1\) as follows: \[\begin{eqnarray*} A_0 &=& \left( \begin{array}{c} A_{01} \\ \tilde{A}_0 \end{array} \right), \quad A_1 = \left( \begin{array}{c} A_{11} \\ \tilde{A}_1 \end{array} \right). \end{eqnarray*}\]

  • ICCs (if storable, otherwise no constraint): \[ \left\{ \begin{array}{ccl} a (e_1) & = & e_1 - e_2 + e_3 \\ b (e_1) &=& 0. \end{array} \right. \]

\(\Leftrightarrow\) The first row of \(A_1\) is \(A_{11} = e_1'- e_2' + e_3'\), the first element of \(A_0\) is \(A_{01} = -\frac{1}{2}\sigma^2_1\) (\(\sigma^2_1\) conditional variance of \(s_{t+1})\).

  • In other words the \(\mathbb{Q}\)-VAR is:

    (where \(\tilde{A}_0\) and \(\tilde{A}_1\) are not constrained).

  • Noting \(y_{t+1} = \log (S_{t+1}/S_t) = s_{t+1} - s_t\), this implies: \[ \mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}_t y_{t+1} = r_t - c_t - \frac{1}{2} \sigma^2_1, \]

    \(y_{t+1} - r_t + c_t + \frac{1}{2} \sigma^2_1\) is a \(\mathbb{Q}\)-martingale difference.

  • Historical dynamics: \[ \begin{array}{lcl} \psi_t (u) & = & \psi^{\mathbb{Q}}_t (u-\alpha_t) - \psi^{\mathbb{Q}}_t (- \alpha_t) \\ &=& u' (A_0 + A_1 w_t) + \frac{1}{2} (u-\alpha_t)' \Sigma (u-\alpha_t)- \frac{1}{2} \alpha'_t \Sigma \alpha_t \\ &=&u' (A_0 + A_1 w_t) - u' \Sigma \alpha_t + \frac{1}{2} u' \Sigma u. \end{array} \]

  • If we take \(\alpha_t = \alpha_0 + \alpha_1 w_t\), we get: \[\begin{eqnarray*} \psi_t (u) &=& u' [A_0 - \Sigma \alpha_0 + (A_1 - \Sigma \alpha_1) w_t] + \frac{1}{2} u' \Sigma u \\ \Rightarrow w_{t+1} &=& A_0 - \Sigma \alpha_0 + (A_1 - \Sigma \alpha_1) w_t + \xi_t,\\ && \xi_t \sim i.i.d. \mathcal{N}(0,\Sigma)\; \mbox{under}\;\mathbb{P}. \end{eqnarray*}\]

  • any VAR(1), with same \(\Sigma\), can be reached.

  • We can also take: \(\alpha_t = \alpha_{0t} + \alpha_1 w_t\). We then get the historical dynamics: \[ w_{t+1} = A_{0} - \Sigma \alpha_{0t} + (A_1 - \Sigma \alpha_1) w_t + \xi_{t+1} \]

  • We can choose \(\alpha_{0t}\) such that: \(A_{0} - \Sigma \alpha_{0t} = \left( \begin{array}{c} \mu_{1t} \\ \mu_2 \\ \mu_3 \end{array}\right)\)

(\(\mu_{1t}, \mu_2, \mu_3\) given). * In particular: \[\begin{eqnarray*} s_{t+1} & = & \mu_{1t} + (A_{11} - \Sigma_1 \alpha_1) w_t + \xi_{1,t+1} \\ & = & \mu_{1t} + \bar{A}_{11} w_t + \xi_{1,t+1}\; \mbox{(say)}, \; \end{eqnarray*}\] \(\bar{A}_{11}\) not constrained, or: \[ s_{t+1} - \nu_{t+1} = \mu_1 + \bar{A}_{11,1} (s_t - \nu_t) + \bar{A}_{11,2} c_t + \bar{A}_{11,3} r_t + \xi_{1,t+1} \] with \[ \mu_{1t} = \mu_1 + \nu_{t+1} - \bar{A}_{11,1} \nu_t. \] * In other words any historical seasonal function \(\nu_t\) can be reached by choosing \(\mu_{1t}\), i.e. \(\alpha_{0t}\).

4.7 Applications

  • Source: Schwartz (1997).
  • Three models whose discrete-time versions are:
  • Model 1: \[\begin{eqnarray*} \mathbb{P}&:& s_{t+1} = a_0 + a_1 s_t + \sigma \varepsilon_{t+1}, \; \; \; \varepsilon_t \stackrel{\mathbb{P}}{\sim} i.i.d. \mathcal{N}(0,1) \\ \mathbb{Q} &:& s_{t+1} = a^*_0 + a_1 s_t + \sigma \xi_{t+1}, \; \; \; \xi_{t} \stackrel{\mathbb{Q}}{\sim} i.i.d. \mathcal{N}(0,1), \end{eqnarray*}\] and \(\log F_{t,T}\) affine functions of \(s_t\).
  • Model 2: \[\begin{eqnarray*} \mathbb{P}&:& s_{t+1} = a_{10} + s_t - c_t + \sigma_1 \varepsilon_{1,t+1}\\ && c_{t+1} = a_{20} + a_{21} c_t + \sigma_2 \varepsilon_{2,t+1}\hspace{1cm} corr (\varepsilon_{1t}, \varepsilon_{2,t}) = \rho \\ \mathbb{Q}&:& s_{t+1} = r - \frac{\sigma^2_1}{2} + s_t - c_t + \sigma_1 \xi_{1,t+1} \\ && c_{t+1} = a^*_{20} + a_{21} c_t + \sigma_2 \xi_{2,t+1} \hspace{1cm} corr (\xi_{1t}, \xi_{2t}) = \rho. \end{eqnarray*}\]
  • Model 3: \[\begin{eqnarray*} \mathbb{P}&:& s_{t+1} = a_{10} + s_t - c_t + r_t + \sigma_1 \varepsilon_{1,t+1} \\ && c_{t+1}= a_{20} + a_{21} c_t + \sigma_2 \varepsilon_{2,t+1} \\ && r_{r+1} = a_{30} + a_{31} r_t + \sigma_3 \varepsilon_{3,t+1} \\ \mathbb{Q}&:& s_{t+1} = -\frac{\sigma^2_1}{2} + s_t - c_t + r_t + \sigma_1 \xi_{1,t+1}\\ &&c_{t+1} = a^*_{20} + a_{21} c_t + \sigma_2 \xi_{2,t+1} \\ &&r_{t+1} = a_{30} + a_{31} r_t + \sigma_3 \xi_{3,t+1}, \end{eqnarray*}\]

\(corr (\varepsilon_{1t}, \varepsilon_{2t}) = \rho_1\), \(corr(\varepsilon_{2t}, \varepsilon_{3t}) = \rho_2\), \(corr(\varepsilon_{1t}, \varepsilon_{3t}) = \rho_3\), idem for the \(\xi_{it}\)’s.

\(\log F_{t,T}\) affine functions of \(s_t, c_t, r_t\).

  • In these models \(s_t, c_t\) are latent, the parameters are estimated by the ML method. The likelihood functions are computed by the Kalman filter.
  • The measurement equations are of the form: \(\log F_{t,T} =\) affine functions of the state variables + error for various values of \(t\) and \(T\).
  • State variables: \(s_t\) or \((s_t, c_t)\), (the \(\mathbb{P}\) and \(\mathbb{Q}\) dynamics of \(r_t\) is estimated separately).

(Constraints between the \(\mathbb{P}\) and \(\mathbb{Q}\) parameters \(\Rightarrow\) helps identification)
